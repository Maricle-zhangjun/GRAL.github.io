Abstract

Robotic garment manipulation under background camouflage remains challenging, as garments often share similar colors and textures with their surrounding environment, leading to severe visual ambiguity and unreliable perception. While affordance learning provides a promising paradigm for inferring actionable regions, existing methods typically rely on large-scale annotations and struggle to generalize to unseen garments. To address this limitation, we adopt a one-shot affordance learning paradigm to enable data-efficient generalization. However, accurate affordance prediction alone is insufficient for reliable garment manipulation, as traditional methods directly selecting the maximum affordance point without considering geometric feasibility often results in unstable grasps, especially on local flat garment regions. Therefore, we further design an affordance-guided manipulation strategy that incorporates geometric awareness to ensure physically feasible and stable execution. Building upon these insights, we propose a Generalizable and Robust Affordance Learning (GRAL) framework for robotic garment manipulation that integrates robust action primitive affordance learning with geometry-aware manipulation strategy, and further construct a Sim-Garment dataset with diverse garment categories and complex background textures to support systematic evaluation. Extensive experiments in both simulation and real-world settings demonstrate that our method significantly improves robustness and generalization, achieving average success rates of 74.29% and 65.71% for garment manipulation tasks. Code and Dataset will be open-sourced after the paper is accepted.


Overview of GRAL framework

Figure 2. Overview of the proposed GRAL framework. Given RGB-D input, OS-APAL learns multimodal representations through geometric-semantic fusion and action prompt-driven feature alignment, enabling robust affordance prediction under background camouflage. Based on the predicted affordance, the affordance-guided manipulation strategy further incorporates geometric cues to refine candidate grasp regions and select stable grasp points, which are then transformed into executable robot actions for reliable garment manipulation across diverse tasks through composing predefined action primitives.


Sim-Garment Dataset

Figure 3. Sim-Garment Dataset. (i) Dataset Collection: diverse garment meshes and textures are randomly rendered with backgrounds to simulate complex garments under background camouflage, followed by multi-angle egocentric RGB-D acquisition. (ii) Dataset Annotation: semantic regions are manually labeled using SAM based on predefined action primitives, and Gaussian heatmaps are generated from region centroids as ground-truth affordance maps. (iii) Statistical Analysis: (a) Example RGB-D samples with diverse garments and background textures. (b) Overview of garment categories and action primitives. (c) Word cloud analysis of action primitives. (d) Garment meshes statistics across categories. (e) Correlation between affordances and garments.
